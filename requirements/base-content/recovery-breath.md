title: "Recovery Breath"
overview: "A deliberate return from an active round back to natural breathing."
---

How to practice:
Use this immediately after a breathing round has finished.
- Take a full inhale at the completion of the last breath.
- Hold briefly and acknowledge the transition.
- Exhale fully with an extended breath to begin down-regulating.
- Let the exhale be through an open or closed mouth.
- Allow the breathing pattern to return to a natural pace and flow.

Purpose:
Used to complete a round without abruptly stopping. The emphasis is transition, recovery, and a conscious return to a natural breath.

Why it works:
The manual presents recovery breath as a variation of conscious breath used intentionally after a round. The held transition and extended exhale help shift the system away from active technique and toward down-regulation and a more natural rhythm.
