id: body-scan
title: "Body Scan"
overview: "A guided inward scan of bodily sensation without judgement."
---

How to practice:
Bring awareness through the body one location at a time.
- Move in an ascending path from the ground up, or in a descending path when grounding is needed.
- Name each location clearly and keep the language simple.
- Use three points of focus for each location: the exact place, where it is in the body, and that same place described from the body's side.
- After locating the area, invite present-moment awareness, softening, acknowledgment, and further ease.
- Begin with a swallow to help shift awareness inward.

Purpose:
Used to tune into the body, reconnect with the physical body, and cultivate attentiveness to the witness of present experience. Relaxation may happen, but the primary goal is not relaxation; it is awareness.

Why it works:
The manual says the practice helps move awareness inward both physiologically and mentally, especially when beginning with a swallow. Clear location-based guidance reduces mental drift and supports investigation, ease, and embodiment without judgement.
