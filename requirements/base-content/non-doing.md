overview: "A quiet inward practice of allowing instead of forcing."
---

Non-doing is the contemplative shift from making experience happen to allowing awareness to deepen without interference.

It is not passivity or laziness. The mind is still present and attentive, but it stops reaching, fixing, and managing. Rather than pushing for a result, attention rests in what is already unfolding.

## What defines it
- Inward rather than outward
- Quietly attentive rather than effortful
- Non-striving rather than goal-driven
- More receptive than controlling

## What it involves
- Letting sensations, thoughts, and moods be as they are
- Allowing awareness to deepen without mental interference
- Reducing the habit of intellectual translation
- Resting in direct experience with less commentary

## What it is like
- A sense of ease and softness in practice
- Less mental grip and less strain
- A more settled, embodied, and simple awareness
- Trust in the depth that appears when effort relaxes

## What belongs here
This is the style of practice that turns inward and becomes established in being. It supports an effortless mind state, reduced attachment to thought, and deeper inward rest.
