The respiratory system is the network of organs and muscles that allows breathing and gas exchange. Its job is to bring oxygen into the body and remove carbon dioxide from the blood.

### Air pathway
- Air enters through the nose or mouth.
- It travels through the throat and into the windpipe.
- From there it moves into two main bronchial tubes, one leading to each lung.
- These tubes divide into smaller bronchioles.
- At the end of the airway network are the alveoli, tiny air sacs where gas exchange occurs.

### What the lungs do
- Oxygen from inhaled air passes into the bloodstream.
- Carbon dioxide, a waste gas, moves from the blood into the lungs and is exhaled.
- This exchange supports cellular respiration, the process that lets the body produce energy from food.

### Structure of the lungs
- The lungs are among the largest organs in the body.
- They contain a highly branched airway system.
- The left lung is slightly smaller because it shares space with the heart.
- The left lung has two lobes; the right lung has three.
- Lung tissue is sponge-like, which lets it expand and contract with breathing.

### How breathing is controlled
- Breathing usually happens without conscious effort.
- Its rate and depth are regulated automatically by homeostatic mechanisms that keep oxygen and carbon dioxide levels stable.
- Breathing rate is a vital sign and is commonly measured in breaths per minute.

### Other functions
- Breathing also supports speech, laughter, and emotional expression.
- It is involved in reflexes such as coughing, yawning, and sneezing.

Important to remember:
- External respiration happens in the lungs.
- Cellular respiration happens in the tissues.
- The whole system exists to support energy production and waste removal.
