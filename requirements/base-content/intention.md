id: "intention"
title: "Intention"
overview: "A purposeful direction that shapes how practice begins and unfolds."
---

Intention is the `why` of the practice. It gives the session a clear direction before any technique begins.

- It gives the practice a meaningful start.
- It narrows attention so your energy is not scattered.
- It supports clarity, coherence, and a sense of purpose.
- It can be personal, professional, or shared when guiding others.

A simple intention can be enough. For example:
- become present
- soften and settle
- cultivate focus
- rest and recover
- support another person with care

In breathwork, meditation, and deep rest, intention clarifies what is being offered and what the practice is trying to support.

Held well, intention gives coherence without becoming pressure.
