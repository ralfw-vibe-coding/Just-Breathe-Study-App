title: "Witness State Practice"
overview: "A meditation practice that trains observation without entanglement."
---

The practice is to remain as the observer while thoughts, sensations, moods, and impulses continue to move.

How to practice:
- notice what is arising
- recognise it without immediately becoming it
- do not chase every thought or emotional hook
- keep returning to the stance of witnessing

Purpose:
- strengthen non-reactive awareness
- reduce identification with passing mental content
- create more space between experience and reaction

Why it works:
- observation interrupts automatic fusion with thoughts and feelings
- the nervous system often settles when less fuel is fed into inner reactivity
- a stronger witness state supports both meditation and everyday self-regulation
