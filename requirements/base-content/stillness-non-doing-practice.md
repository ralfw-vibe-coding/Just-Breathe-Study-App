Stillness is a deliberate reduction of stimulation so the body and mind can settle into deep rest and quiet awareness.

### What it involves
- **Sit**: create the conditions for rest with a comfortable, supported posture.
- **Settle**: allow remaining movement, energy, and mental activity to soften.
- **Be still**: reduce distractions, close the eyes or use a soft gaze if needed, and give yourself time to arrive.

### How to practice it
- Choose a quiet, settled space.
- Remove distractions and temptations where possible.
- Let the breath be natural rather than trying to control it.
- Notice body and mind without judging the experience.
- Stay long enough for deep rest to emerge, then return gently.

### Why it matters
Stillness supports awareness, regulation, and recovery. It gives the mind and body time to be at ease completely and keeps the return to the outer environment from being rushed.

### Keep in mind
Stillness is a commitment to less, not a dramatic achievement. It is practised through gentleness, patience, and enough time to let deep rest emerge.
