Focused Intention is the meditation depth that directs attention toward a concept, image, meaning, story, or emotional quality.

These practices often work through:

- narrative and story
- imagery and visualisation
- heartfelt meaning or spiritual connection
- repeated phrases, sounds, or affirming language

These practices often begin in the head and become felt in the heart. The intellect is still being engaged, but in a more guided and meaningful way.

Common practices in this group include:

- loving-kindness and metta bhavana, which direct goodwill toward self and others
- ajapa-japa, which uses the natural rhythm of mantra repetition
- visualisation, which works with mental imagery
- guided or intention-led meditation, which uses spoken narrative and direction
- mantras, which focus the mind through repeated sacred sound or phrase
- affirmations, which repeat supportive statements to reinforce a chosen state

Purpose:
- quiet the mind through meaningful direction
- create heartfelt connection to an idea, quality, or emotion
- use imagination, wisdom, or mantra as a steadying force
