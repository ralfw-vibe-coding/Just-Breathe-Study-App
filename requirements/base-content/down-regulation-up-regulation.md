title: "Down Regulation / Up Regulation"
overview: "A way to tell calming breath work from energizing breath work."
---

These terms describe the direction a breathing practice is meant to move the nervous system.

- **Down regulation** supports calming, soothing, grounding, and balancing.
- **Up regulation** supports greater activation, alertness, and energy.

Use the distinction to match the breath to the state someone is in:

- When a person feels tense, overwhelmed, scattered, or overstimulated, choose work that down regulates.
- When a person feels dull, collapsed, lethargic, or under-activated, choose work that up regulates.

The direction matters as much as the technique. A method is not chosen only because it exists, but because the system needs to be settled or awakened.

Important to remember:
- down regulation reduces activation
- up regulation increases activation
- the right choice depends on the state that is present now
