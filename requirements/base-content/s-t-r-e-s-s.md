title: "S.T.R.E.S.S."
overview: "Six common everyday triggers that create pressure and nervous-system reactivity."
---

Stress is described in the manual as the body's response to pressure, especially when something new, unexpected, or hard to control threatens the sense of self.

The six letters point to six common everyday triggers:
- Situational Occurrences: unplanned or disruptive moments that knock us off course
- Time: pressure created when needed time and available time do not match
- Responsibilities: real or perceived obligations to tasks, people, and circumstances
- Emotions: personal reactions to events and stimulus, which differ from person to person
- Societal Expectation: the load of other people's opinions, norms, and assumptions
- Sleep: the quantity and quality of rest that determine how much pressure can be managed well

Important to remember:
- Stress becomes more disruptive when it accumulates in the nervous system.
- Managing stress begins with recognising what is actually creating it.
