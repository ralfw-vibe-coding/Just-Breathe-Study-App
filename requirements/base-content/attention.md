title: "Attention"
overview: "The deliberate directing of the mind within practice."
---

Attention is the deliberate placing of the mind on one chosen point. Where awareness is broad, attention is selective.

In practice, attention is often directed inward. Helpful ways to support this shift include:

- closing the eyes, or softening the gaze if that feels more appropriate
- becoming still and reducing unnecessary movement
- simplifying the environment and limiting distractions
- returning to the present moment whenever the mind wanders

This is not rigid concentration for its own sake. It is a calm, repeatable return from distraction toward what matters now.

Placed well, attention reduces mental spread and gives practice shape.
