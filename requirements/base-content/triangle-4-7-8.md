title: "Triangle 4-7-8"
overview: "An anxiety-focused triangle-breathing variation with a longer hold and exhale."
---

How to practice:
Use triangle breathing as the base pattern.
- Inhale for a count of 4.
- Pause and hold for a count of 7.
- Exhale for a count of 8.
- Repeat for 10-12 rounds or about 2 minutes.

Purpose:
Used for managing anxiety and quelling errant stress responses. The emphasis is strengthening the mind-body connection through a deliberately slowed ratio.

Why it works:
The manual presents this Andrew Weil variation as especially effective for anxiety because the longer rhythm helps fortify the connection between mind and body. As the breathing slows and attention stays fully on the count, the parasympathetic nervous system is described as becoming engaged.
