title: "Resting Position / Supported Practice"
overview: "A supported posture that helps the body give way to rest."
---

The purpose of a supported resting position is to remove unnecessary effort so the body can settle more completely.

What matters:
- comfort
- stability
- enough support that the body does not keep bracing
- enough ease to remain without strain

Supported practice becomes especially important when:
- the practice is long
- the body is tired or tense
- sitting upright would create distraction or extra effort
- deep rest is the main aim

When the position is well chosen, less attention is spent managing discomfort and more is available for unwinding.
