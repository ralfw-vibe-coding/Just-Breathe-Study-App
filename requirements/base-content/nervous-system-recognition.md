Recognition is the skill of reading the state before trying to change the state.

What is being recognised:
- the present breathing pattern
- signs of stress or settling
- hyperstimulation, depletion, or overwhelm
- capacity, tolerance, and readiness
- whether the need is direct response, gradual response, or simply support and observation

This matters because no single breathing technique suits every condition. The breath has to be matched to the state that is actually present.

Distinction:
- Recognition notices what is here.
- Regulation applies a method to influence it.
- Release describes what may move once the system begins to let go.

Recognition is the assessment layer that makes later breathwork intelligent.
