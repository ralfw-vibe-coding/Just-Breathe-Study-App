title: "Visualisation"
overview: "A guided inner narrative that moves from intentional thinking into embodied feeling."
---

How to practice:
Use a controlled narrative to guide awareness into a specific imagined place, idea, or perspective.
- Set the pace and flow carefully.
- Build the scene through details and considerations.
- Move from who, what, when, where, and why toward a more embodied sense of where, when, what, and how it is being felt.
- Let the imagined experience become sensory and lived rather than only conceptual.

Purpose:
Used especially for students who are new to meditation or struggle to sit without gentle distraction. The emphasis is meaningful inner exploration that carries attention away from everything else.

Why it works:
The manual describes visualisation as a movement from intentional thinking to embodied feeling. A strong narrative holds the mind's curiosity while gradually shifting it from intellectual structuring into felt sensory experience.
