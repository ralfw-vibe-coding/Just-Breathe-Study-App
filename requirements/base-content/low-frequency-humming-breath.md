overview: "A gentle humming exhale for soothing the nervous system."
---

How to practice:
Gently place the thumbs over each tragus with light pressure.
- Let the remaining fingers rest naturally along the forehead.
- With the mouth closed, inhale slowly to full capacity.
- Exhale slowly while creating a gentle humming sound.
- Repeat as needed.

Purpose:
Used as a gentle down-regulation practice that soothes the nervous system and frees the mind of agitation. The emphasis is vibration, softness, and a calming exhale.

Why it works:
The manual states that the vibrations produced by the practice are known to soothe the nervous system and quiet agitation. The combination of closed-mouth breathing and low-frequency sound gives the exhale a steady, settling quality.
