title: "Conscious Breath"
overview: "A slow, considered breath for centering and immediate awareness."
---

How to practice:
Sit or stand and bring attention completely onto the breath.
- Take a conscious inhale without needing to make it especially deep.
- Exhale with ease through an open or closed mouth.
- Repeat as needed.
- Continue for 10 rounds or about 60 seconds.

Purpose:
Used at the beginning of practice or whenever a reset into clear present-moment awareness is needed. The emphasis is quality, intention, and centering rather than quantity.

Why it works:
The manual describes this as a quality inhale and exhale that signals focus and intention. Deliberately bringing attention onto one steady breath helps gather awareness into the body and reset the system into the moment.
