title: "R.E.S.T."
overview: "A simple model for what real rest requires."
---

Rest is described as a conscious state of healing and recovery. In the manual, REST is defined as the reduction of external stimulus over a prolonged period of time.

The four letters point to four key conditions:
- Reduce: intentionally do less and reduce noise, distraction, and demand
- External: reduce what is outside the self and constantly pulling attention outward
- Stimulus: reduce sensory, social, cognitive, and emotional input that keeps the system activated
- Time: allow enough time for unwinding, because a brief pause is not the same as meaningful rest

Important to remember:
- Rest and sleep are related but not identical.
- Rest requires deliberate reduction, not only the absence of activity.
