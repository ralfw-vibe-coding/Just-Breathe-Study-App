id: "3-gratitudes"
title: "3 Gratitudes"
overview: "A closing ritual of three intentional breaths."
---

Three intentional breaths complete the practice with acknowledgement, gratitude, and a clear emotional landing.

1. Breath for you
- Acknowledge yourself for taking the time to practice.
- Honour the effort and commitment it took to arrive here fully.
- Recognise yourself as you are, right now.

2. Breath for support
- Offer gratitude for the people, conditions, or forms of support that sustain you.
- This may be loved ones, teachers, community, a group, or anything that helps carry your well-being.

3. Breath for this moment
- Appreciate this moment exactly as it is.
- Let the final breath seal in the quiet of the practice and the sense of time well spent.

Why it matters:
- It gives the practice a ritual ending rather than an abrupt stop.
- It shifts the mind from effort into appreciation.
- It helps the state of practice land with sincerity rather than drift away.

Important to remember:
- The first breath is for you.
- The second breath is for support.
- The third breath is for this moment.
- The closing is simple, but it should feel meaningful.
