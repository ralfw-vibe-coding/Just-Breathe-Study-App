The thinking mind is active, interpretive, restless, and often crowded. Meditation begins by understanding this activity instead of fighting it blindly.

When attention is pulled into thought, the task is to notice that movement clearly. This creates a useful distinction:

- **Attention** is the place the mind is drawn to
- **Awareness** is the wider field that notices what is happening
- **Witnessing** is the ability to observe mental activity without being carried away by it

This changes meditation from thought-suppression into recognition. The aim is not to stop the mind by force, but to see mental activity clearly enough that it stops ruling the whole experience.

This is the doorway into witness state, present moment awareness, and deeper non-doing practices.
