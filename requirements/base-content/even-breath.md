title: "Even Breath"
overview: "Equal-length inhale and exhale for calm and baseline regulation."
---

How to practice:
Find a comfortable seat and notice the current pace of the breath.
- Take a conscious breath to begin.
- Inhale slowly for a count of 4 or a similar slow count.
- Take a natural pause of about 1 second at the top.
- Exhale for the same count of 4.
- Continue for 30 rounds or about 2 minutes.
- Adjust the count if needed so the pattern creates calm and ease.
- Release the technique and return to a natural breath when complete.

Purpose:
Used to soothe body and mind, slow the breathing cycle, and establish a comprehensive baseline for later techniques. The emphasis is steadiness and ease.

Why it works:
The manual describes even breath as steady inhalation and exhalation of equal duration that helps down-regulate the nervous system. Equal timing slows the cycle and gives the body a simple, non-forceful rhythm to follow.
