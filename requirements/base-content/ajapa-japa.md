---
id: ajapa-japa
title: "Ajapa-Japa"
overview: "Quiet repetition of a mantra with continuous awareness."
---

How to practice:
Choose a mantra and repeat it quietly to yourself with dedicated attention.
- Stay with the mantra continuously rather than only thinking about its material meaning.
- For many practitioners, hold the mantra gently rather than trying to force a perfect no-thinking state.
- A common form is repeating the mantra 108 times.
- A mala bead can be used to keep track.
- Keep attention on the full embodiment of the affirmation being offered.

Purpose:
Used to stabilize awareness through continuous mantra repetition and deepen connection to the affirmation being offered. The emphasis is dedicated attention, continuity, and embodiment.

Why it works:
The manual explains ajapa-japa as japa without the same level of intellectual effort needed to relentlessly hold the mantra's material nature. Repetition keeps awareness steady, while the sustained mantra gradually shifts the practice from mental saying toward fuller embodied presence.
