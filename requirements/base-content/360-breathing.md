title: "360 Breathing"
overview: "Full-torso breathing that emphasizes circumferential expansion."
---

How to practice:
Find a comfortable seat with one hand on the belly and one hand on the chest.
- Breathe slowly and fully into both belly and chest equally.
- Feel both hands rise and expand.
- Focus on expansion moving evenly in a 360 degree direction.
- Pause at the top for about 1 second.
- Exhale slowly and let belly and chest empty.
- Repeat for 10 rounds or about 60 seconds.

Purpose:
Used to encourage a fuller breath, even rib-cage expansion, and greater torso breath capacity. The emphasis is distribution and fullness rather than speed or intensity.

Why it works:
The manual describes 360 breathing as a circumferential variation of belly and chest breathing that encourages full breath and oxygen distribution with even rib-cage inflation. The all-around expansion helps the practitioner feel the whole torso participating in the breath.
