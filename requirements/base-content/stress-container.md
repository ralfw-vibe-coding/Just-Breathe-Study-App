id: stress-container
title: "Stress Container"
overview: "A model for how stress accumulates, overflows, and is released."
---

The stress container represents the level of vulnerability a person carries and the amount of everyday stress that can be held before overwhelm develops.

A larger container suggests lower vulnerability and greater capacity. A smaller container overflows more quickly, especially when shaped by factors such as trauma, bullying, abuse, or chronic pressure.

The model also includes:
- a tap, through which stress can be released
- trapped stress below the tap, which may not be easy to discharge immediately
- helpful coping strategies, which keep the tap working
- unhelpful coping strategies, which block release and increase build-up

Important to remember:
- Stress containers are never completely empty.
- The goal is active management, not total elimination of stress.
