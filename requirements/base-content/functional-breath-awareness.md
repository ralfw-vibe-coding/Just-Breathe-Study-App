Functional breath awareness is the physiological starting point of breathwork. Before changing the breath, learn to read it.

What to observe:
- where the breath is located in the body
- whether it is diaphragmatic, thoracic, clavicular, or mixed
- whether the inhale and exhale are smooth, strained, noisy, shallow, held, or rushed
- whether the rhythm is steady or reactive
- whether the breath is nasal or oral
- whether the body looks regulated, braced, fatigued, or overstimulated

What this tells you:
- how the respiratory system is currently functioning
- whether the breath is efficient or compensatory
- how the nervous system may be expressing stress through breathing
- what kind of intervention is likely to help

Why it matters:
- Breathwork becomes more precise when it begins with observation.
- Recognition of the pattern comes before manipulation of the pattern.
- This is the bridge between respiratory physiology and practical guidance.

Important to remember:
- Functional awareness is not yet a state-change technique.
- Its job is to clarify how the person is breathing now.
- Better observation leads to better regulation choices.
