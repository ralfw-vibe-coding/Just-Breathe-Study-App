title: "Loving Kindness / Metta Bhavana"
overview: "A five-stage cultivation of love, kindness, and goodwill."
---

How to practice:
Move through the five traditional stages of the practice.
- Begin with yourself and cultivate peace, calm, strength, confidence, and love.
- Bring to mind someone you love and extend the same well-wishing toward them.
- Include someone neutral or an acquaintance and reflect on their humanity.
- Include someone difficult or an enemy and send love equally without feeding hatred.
- Extend loving-kindness outward to everyone around you and finally to all beings everywhere.

Purpose:
Used to cultivate loving-kindness as a felt quality of the heart. The emphasis is expanding the range of love from the self outward without losing sincerity or steadiness.

Why it works:
The manual describes metta as an emotion felt in the heart and bhavana as cultivation or development. Repetition through the five stages gradually broadens the emotional field so goodwill becomes less selective and more stable.
