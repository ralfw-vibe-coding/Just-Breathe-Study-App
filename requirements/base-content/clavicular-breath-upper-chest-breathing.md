title: "Clavicular Breath / Upper Chest Breathing"
overview: "Upper-chest breathing that lifts the rib cage through the chest and neck muscles."
---

How to practice:
Begin by noticing the natural state of the breath.
- Breathe into the upper chest and lower throat space.
- Feel the expansion in a 360 direction rather than only up and down.
- Repeat for 10 rounds or about 60 seconds.

Purpose:
Used to sense the uppermost breathing pattern and recognize how the shoulders, upper chest, and throat can become involved. The emphasis is awareness of high chest breathing rather than relaxation.

Why it works:
The manual states that clavicular breathing relies on the muscles of the chest, shoulders, upper back, and neck to elevate the upper rib cage. Practising it consciously makes that compensatory pattern easier to identify and differentiate from diaphragmatic or costal breathing.
