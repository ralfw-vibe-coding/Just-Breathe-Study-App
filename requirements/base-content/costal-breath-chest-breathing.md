title: "Costal Breath / Chest Breathing"
overview: "Chest-focused breathing that emphasizes the intercostal muscles."
---

How to practice:
Find a comfortable seat or symmetrical standing position.
- Notice the current pace of the breath.
- Take a conscious breath to begin.
- Breathe slowly into the chest and feel the expansion of the intercostal muscles.
- Exhale slowly and let the chest empty.
- Repeat for 10 rounds or about 60 seconds.

Purpose:
Used to bring awareness to chest-dominant breathing mechanics. The emphasis is recognizing how the ribs and chest participate in the breath.

Why it works:
The manual explains that costal breathing requires contraction of the intercostal muscles and that air leaves the lungs passively as those muscles relax. Practising it deliberately makes the mechanics of shallow or chest-led breathing easier to feel and understand.
