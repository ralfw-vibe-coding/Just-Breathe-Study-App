id: squatted-belly
title: "Squatted Belly"
overview: "A lowered stance that gives the belly more room to move during active breathing."
---

The position lowers the body so the abdomen can soften, expand, and work more freely during stronger breath practices.

How to use it:
- come into a squat or a deeply lowered position that feels stable
- allow the belly to relax rather than brace
- use the stance with energising or abdominally active breathwork when extra access to the lower torso is helpful

Purpose:
- improve access to belly movement
- reduce unnecessary restriction during active breathing
- support stronger respiratory effort from a grounded base

Why it helps:
- posture changes what parts of the body are free to move
- a lowered stance can make abdominal movement easier to feel and use
- grounded lower-body support often helps the upper body work with less rigidity
