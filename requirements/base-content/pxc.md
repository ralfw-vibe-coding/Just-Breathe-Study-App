Use this when stress, anxiety, overwhelm, or overstimulation is building and you need a quick shift toward calm.

## Purpose
This protocol is designed to dissolve stress fast. It supports the body’s relaxation response by down regulating the sympathetic nervous system, making it useful as a direct response rather than a gradual practice.

## How the techniques work together
- **Physiological sigh**: creates an immediate down regulating effect and helps interrupt the stress pattern.
- **Extended exhale**: slows the pace and lengthens the out-breath, helping activate the parasympathetic nervous system and ease tension.
- **Coherent breathing**: steadies the breath into a balanced rhythm so calm can settle and be maintained.

## Practice flow
1. Begin with physiological sighing to quickly reduce arousal.
2. Continue with extended exhales to deepen relaxation.
3. Finish with coherent breathing to stabilize the nervous system.

## When to use it
- During acute stress
- When feeling anxious or overwhelmed
- After overstimulation
- As a short reset before returning to activity

The sequence moves from interruption, to decompression, to stabilization.
