The closing sequence is a deliberate landing rather than an abrupt stop.

The core steps are:

1. Conscious breath to close
Use a conscious breath, sometimes called a recovery breath, as the point of closure or transition:
- take one last breath together
- use an even, full, or sighing release
- allow the breath to return toward its natural pattern

2. Cultivate awareness
Return to witness and observation:
- recognise the current state of experience
- notice how you feel now
- do not judge or qualify the sensations

3. Finish in stillness
Let the session settle before moving on.

4. Close with 3 Gratitudes
Use the ritual closing of three intentional breaths:
- a breath for you
- a breath for support
- a breath for this moment

Important to remember:
- Closing helps the practice land in the body and mind.
- Gratitude is not an optional extra in the method; it is named as a ritual closing.
- The final transition should preserve ease rather than break it suddenly.
