title: "Opposite Alternate Nostril Breathing — Exhale"
overview: "An alternate-nostril pattern that exhales through one side at a time."
---

**How to practice**
1. Sit comfortably and lift the right hand to the nose.
2. Inhale through both nostrils.
3. Use the ring finger to close the left nostril and exhale only through the right.
4. Release the seal and inhale again through both nostrils.
5. Close the right nostril with the thumb and exhale only through the left.
6. This completes one round. Continue for about 10 rounds.

**Purpose**
- promote patience
- improve focus and control
- relieve stress and anxiety
- make the exhale more deliberate and regulated

**Why it works**
- the shared inhale keeps the intake simple while the alternating exhale increases conscious control
- one-sided exhalation slows the release and makes the practitioner more aware of pacing
- the combination of rhythm, nasal breathing, and controlled alternation helps settle scattered attention
