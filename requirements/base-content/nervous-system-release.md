Release is what begins to happen when tension, activation, or emotional charge is no longer only contained, but starts to move.

It may show up as:
- emotional release
- a fuller body response
- unwinding after prolonged hyperstimulation
- a shift into expanded or non-ordinary states
- the sense that the system is discharging something it has been holding

This is different from regulation:
- regulation aims for steadiness and control
- release describes the movement that can happen once the system opens enough

Why safety matters:
- release should not be forced for its own sake
- the body still needs recovery and orientation
- intense processes need support, pacing, and a clear way back to steadiness

When handled well, release can make deeper integration and rest possible.
