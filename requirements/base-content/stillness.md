Stillness is more than the absence of movement. It is a deliberate move toward less stimulation, less distraction, and less outward pull.

Stillness supports:
- arrival at the start of practice
- deeper rest during practice
- gentler integration at the end

It can be physical, mental, and sensory:
- the body becomes less busy
- the mind becomes less outwardly engaged
- the environment is allowed to quieten around the practice

Stillness is one of the conditions that makes inwardness possible.
