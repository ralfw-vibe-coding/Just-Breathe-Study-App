How to practice:
Breathe with a slow, even rhythm.
- Inhale into the belly for about five seconds.
- Exhale for about five seconds.
- Continue for three to five minutes.
- Keep the breath natural rather than robotic or exaggerated.

Purpose:
Used to create a steady rhythm and increase heart rate variability. The emphasis is coherence, natural slowness, and nervous-system regulation through pacing.

Why it works:
The manual defines coherent breathing as breathing at about 5.5 breaths per minute and states that its purpose is to increase heart rate variability. The slower, even pace gives the cardiovascular and respiratory systems a more coherent rhythm to follow.
