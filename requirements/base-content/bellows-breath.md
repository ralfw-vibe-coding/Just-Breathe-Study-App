title: "Bellows Breath"
overview: "A forceful abdominal-and-diaphragmatic breath for heat and energy."
---

How to practice:
Find a comfortable seat.
- Take a long inhale and exhale through the nose.
- Inhale again and exhale by quickly contracting the abdominal muscles.
- Follow this with a quick diaphragmatic inhale so the breath moves in and out in quick succession.
- Continue for about one minute.
- Finish with one long slow inhale through the nose and one slow exhale through the mouth.

Purpose:
Used to strengthen the abdomen, drain excess mucus from the lungs, and energise body and mind. The emphasis is active inhalation and exhalation driven by the abdominals and diaphragm.

Why it works:
The manual describes both sides of the breath as active contractions of the abdominals and diaphragm. That strong respiratory pumping creates heat, mobilises the torso, and produces the energising effect the technique is known for.
