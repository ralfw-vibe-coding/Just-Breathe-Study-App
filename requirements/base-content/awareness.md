Awareness is the broad field in which experience is noticed. It includes breath, body, thought, sensation, mood, and environment before anything is narrowed into a specific focus.

## How it differs from attention and intention
- **Awareness** is spacious and receptive. It holds what is present.
- **Attention** is narrower and more specific. It selects one thing to focus on.
- **Intention** is the choice that directs attention or shapes how you respond.

## What awareness makes possible
- Recognizing what is arising in the moment
- Noticing patterns without immediate reaction
- Including both inner experience and outer stimulus
- Creating a clear starting point for breath practice, meditation, and regulation

## In practice
A simple way to work with awareness is to pause and observe without changing anything. You might notice:
- the movement of breathing
- tension or ease in the body
- mental activity
- emotional tone
- sounds, silence, or other environmental cues

This is why awareness is foundational. It is the ground from which attention can be placed and intention can be expressed with clarity.
