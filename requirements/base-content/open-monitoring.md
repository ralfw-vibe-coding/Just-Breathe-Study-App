id: open-monitoring
title: "Open Monitoring"
overview: "A witnessing practice that stays open to whatever arises."
---

Open monitoring does not stay with only one fixed object. Awareness remains broad and notices breath, sensation, thoughts, feeling, and change as they arise.

How to practice:
- settle first so attention is not scattered
- remain open rather than narrowing to one object
- notice what appears without immediately following it
- allow experience to come and go inside a stable field of awareness

Purpose:
- strengthen the witness state
- keep awareness broad without losing presence
- reduce attachment to any one mental event

Why it works:
- the mind learns observation without constant grasping
- thoughts are seen as events rather than commands
- broad awareness reduces over-identification with whatever is loudest in the moment
