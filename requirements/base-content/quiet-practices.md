In the manual, quiet practices are the category used for practices that down-regulate the nervous system, de-stress the body, and support response-driven behaviour.

They are not limited to one modality. Breathwork, meditation, deep rest, and other settling practices all belong here when they help reduce stimulation and guide the system toward ease.

Their purpose is broader than stress relief alone. Quiet practices are described as supporting five critical pillars of well-being:
- Physical
- Mental & Emotional
- Social
- Spiritual
- Environmental

Important to remember:
- Quiet practices are defined by their down-regulating effect.
- They are meant to be everyday supports, not rare interventions.
- Their value is measured by how they help a person soften, settle, and live better.
