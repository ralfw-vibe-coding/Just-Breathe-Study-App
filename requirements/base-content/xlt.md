XLT is a calming protocol for sustained rest. It is best used when the aim is not only to stop stress quickly, but to settle into a more durable parasympathetic response.

### How the techniques work together
- **Extended Exhale** begins the process by making the out-breath longer than the in-breath. This helps release tension quickly and soften arousal.
- **Low Frequency Humming Breath** adds a steady humming vibration on the exhale. This can further settle the breath rhythm and support a deeper sense of calm.
- **Triangle 4-7-8** brings a structured breathing pattern that reinforces regulation through clear timing and a restful pace.

### What it is for
- calming the nervous system
- reducing the intensity of stress
- supporting relaxation and decompression
- encouraging slower, more controlled breathing
- helping the body shift into sustained rest

### When to use it
Use this sequence when you want a restorative breathing practice that gradually lowers stress and supports recovery, rather than an energising or activating breath practice.

The sequence moves from release, to soothing, to structured calm.
