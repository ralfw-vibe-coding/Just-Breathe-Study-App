title: "Close your eyes, be still, just breathe"
overview: "The community invocation used to begin practice with simplicity and shared meaning."
---

How to practice:
Let the phrase set the entry into practice one instruction at a time.
- Close the eyes or soften the gaze if that feels safer.
- Let the body become more still.
- Allow the breath to become the main point of attention.
- Pause long enough for the transition into practice to be felt.

Purpose:
Used to mark the shift from ordinary activity into deliberate practice. The emphasis is arrival, simplification, and inward attention.

Why it works:
A clear opening cue reduces sensory spread and gives the nervous system a recognizable transition signal. Softening visual input, reducing movement, and turning toward the breath all support parasympathetic settling and stronger attentional coherence.
