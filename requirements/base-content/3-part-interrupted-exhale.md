title: "3-Part Interrupted Exhale"
overview: "A staged exhale that lengthens the release and explores lung emptying."
---

**How to practice**
1. Find a comfortable seated or reclined position.
2. Take a few clearing breaths.
3. Inhale fully to a comfortable capacity.
4. Exhale one third of the breath.
5. Take a short pause.
6. Exhale the next third.
7. Take another short pause.
8. Exhale the final third, or until the lungs feel empty.
9. Take a final short pause.
10. Inhale fully.

**Purpose**
- lengthen the exhale
- understand the true capacity of the lungs
- improve control over the release phase of the breath
- support calming and down-regulation through a more deliberate exhale

**Why it works**
- segmenting the exhale increases awareness of how the breath leaves the body
- the added pauses slow the rhythm and reduce impulsive breathing
- a more complete, controlled exhale often helps the system settle and release tension
