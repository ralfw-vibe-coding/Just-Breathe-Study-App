Present Moment Awareness is the depth of practice that places awareness on what is unfolding now, inwardly and directly.

What belongs here:
- Body-based scanning practices that move attention through physical sensations
- Breath-based awareness practices that stay with the inhale, exhale, and subtle breathing sensations
- Sensory practices that rest on what is seen, heard, felt, tasted, or smelled in the immediate moment
- Focused gazing practices such as candle gazing or cloud watching
- Practices that observe mental patterns as they arise without following them
- Awareness of the body’s layers or dimensions of experience, such as the koshas

What defines it:
- Requires steady, detailed attention
- Often feels like relentless attention
- Observes physical sensation or mental pattern as it unfolds
- Can still use an outward object, such as focused gaze, when the awareness remains fully in the here and now

Purpose:
- train witness-based observation
- reduce intellectual translation
- develop intuitive self-awareness and embodiment
- keep attention in real-time experience
