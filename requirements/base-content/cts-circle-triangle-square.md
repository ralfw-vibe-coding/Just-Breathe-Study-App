CTS means Circle, Triangle, Square. It is a foundational balanced protocol built from three simple breathing patterns that progressively increase structure.

## Purpose
- supports autonomic balance
- improves respiratory efficiency
- helps coordinate inhale, pause, exhale, and retention
- can create a smoother, more regulated breathing cycle
- provides a base for more advanced breathwork

## How the practices work together
- **Even Breath** is the starting point. Inhalation and exhalation are the same length, making it the simplest way to establish steadiness.
- **Triangle Breathing** introduces a three-part rhythm with pauses, adding structure and greater control.
- **Box Breathing** extends that pattern into four equal phases, reinforcing consistency, calm, and rhythmic regulation.

## What the sequence develops
- even pacing
- a sense of balance in the breath
- stronger breath control
- a more efficient, settled breathing pattern

Suggested progression:
1. establish steadiness with Even Breath
2. add three-part control with Triangle Breathing
3. deepen structure and clarity with Box Breathing

This makes CTS a simple way to optimise autonomic balance while giving the practitioner a clear breathing foundation.
