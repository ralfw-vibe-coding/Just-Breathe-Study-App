title: "Diaphragmatic Breathing"
overview: "Deep belly breathing that fully engages the diaphragm."
---

How to practice:
Find a comfortable seat or a symmetrical standing position.
- Notice the current pace of the breath.
- Take a conscious breath to begin.
- Place one hand on the belly and one hand on the chest.
- Breathe slowly and fully into the belly and let it expand.
- Pause at the top for about 1 second.
- Exhale slowly and let the belly empty.
- Repeat for 10 rounds or about 60 seconds.

Purpose:
Used as a foundational regulation technique and as a calming response during stress or anxiousness. The emphasis is nervous-system regulation and a parasympathetic response.

Why it works:
The manual describes diaphragmatic breathing as fully engaging the diaphragm and increasing the efficiency of the lungs. That fuller diaphragmatic action supports better breathing mechanics and is presented as a direct way to regulate the nervous system and create a calming effect.
