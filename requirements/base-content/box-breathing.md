How to practice:
Find a comfortable seat and notice the current pace of the breath.
- Take a conscious breath to begin.
- Inhale slowly for a count of 4.
- Hold at the top for a count of 4.
- Exhale slowly for a count of 4.
- Continue for 30 rounds or about 2 minutes.
- Adjust the count if needed so the pattern remains supportive.
- Release the technique and return to a natural breath when complete.

Purpose:
Used to reduce physical stress symptoms and increase mental clarity. The emphasis is equal structure across all four parts of the breath.

Why it works:
The manual describes box breathing as a technique in which inhalation, internal retention, exhalation, and external retention are all of equal length. That equal structure creates a stable rhythm that supports steadiness, pacing, and clearer concentration.
