overview: "A sequence that uses breath regulation first, then meditation, then quiet stillness."
---

This combination starts with a nervous-system regulation protocol, then moves into meditation, and ends in stillness.

Purpose:
- respond to a clear need state through selected breathwork
- carry the regulated state into meditation while the system is more workable
- finish with stillness so the effect can stabilise without more doing

Why the sequence works:
- NSRT provides a targeted regulatory intervention at the start
- meditation becomes more accessible when the body is less activated
- stillness at the end gives the nervous system time to integrate the shift without immediately re-engaging effort

This sequence is especially useful when a person needs more targeted breathwork than a pure meditation entry, but does not need the full length of a complete unwinding format.
