title: "Experiential Breathwork"
overview: "Breathing practice focused on direct felt experience and deeper state shifts."
---

Experiential breathwork is organised around what is felt, revealed, and moved through the body-mind, not only around a narrow regulation target.

Its emphasis is on:
- full body-mind connection
- conscious awareness during the experience
- somatic introspection
- expanded states or non-ordinary states of consciousness
- emotional release and deeper inner process

This style of breathwork is less about "apply this exact ratio for this exact outcome" and more about staying inside the lived experience of the breath as it changes sensation, perception, emotion, and state.

Compared with systematic breathwork:
- systematic work is chosen for a clear, repeatable effect
- experiential work is chosen for depth of process and direct experience

It often belongs in practices where breath becomes immersive, continuous, exploratory, or transformational. The practitioner is not only shaping the breath, but learning through what the breath reveals.
