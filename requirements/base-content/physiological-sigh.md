How to practice:
Begin by noticing the current quality of the breath.
- Take a full inhale through the nose.
- Take a second inhale through the nose at a smaller capacity, filling the intake to maximum.
- Pause briefly at the top.
- Open the mouth and sigh out with an extended exhale.
- Repeat for 5-10 rounds.

Purpose:
Used to respond to stress, anxiety, overwhelm, or over-stimulation. The emphasis is rapid down-regulation and a quick shift out of an activated stress response.

Why it works:
The manual describes the physiological sigh as a simple and effective down-regulating stress-response breath. In the protocol material it is also used to activate the body's relaxation response by helping to reduce sympathetic over-activation through the long sighing exhale.
